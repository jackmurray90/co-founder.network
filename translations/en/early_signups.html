Early sign-up incentive

Monetization strategy for this website is to eventually charge $200 a month for the ability to create job listings on the site and match them with potential candidates. Obviously, the value proposition for potential customers only exists if there are a number of existing users of the platform.

To solve this chicken-and-egg problem, we are undertaking three measures:

  1. Onboard high value users early.
  2. Focus on a restricted market.
  3. Offer free job listings in the beginning.

To incentivise early sign-ups of high value users, we are offering 0.05%% of annual profits each to the first 1000 people who add their CV to the site, paid on December 31st each year up to and including December 31 2035. To restrict our market, we are making the offer only available to software engineers in San Francisco or the Bay Area.

Assuming we can get at least 10 paying customers each year, this will be at least $12 per year for a total of at least $156 by 2036.

It should be noted that if we can get at least 1000 users on the website it is possible that it will be far more successful due to the network effect, resulting in more user sign-ups and 100x or more paying customers. So, there is a small chance that adding your CV to the site at this early stage could result in $15.6k or more by 2036. Please take this into consideration.

It is assumed that if you are a software engineer in San Francisco who can understand this incentivization scheme, you are already a high-value user.
